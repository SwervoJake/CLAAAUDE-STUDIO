# Faction Reference Guide
## Status: LOCKED

## 🏛️ The Architects

**Role in the city:** They built it. They run the infrastructure. They believe the city is their creation and therefore their responsibility — and their right.

**Personality:** Cold, brilliant, paternalistic. They are not cruel. They genuinely believe they know best. That is what makes them dangerous.

**Aesthetic:** White. Geometric. Oppressively clean. Their zone of the city feels like a hospital crossed with a government building. Everything works perfectly. Everything is watched.

**What they offer players:**
- Best tech upgrades for gear
- Access to city infrastructure systems (in later versions)
- Lore about how the city was actually built (and what was sacrificed)

**Reputation unlocks:**
- Trusted: Architect-grade tech mods, extended NPC dialogue about city history
- Allied: Access to restricted Architect research wing, the truth about the city's founding

**Faction motto:** "We built this. We decide what it becomes."

**Relationship with other factions:**
- Vanguard: Tense alliance. The Architects need the Vanguard to maintain order. The Vanguard tolerates the Architects because they control resources. Neither likes the arrangement.
- Hollow: Quiet hostility. The Architects do not acknowledge the Hollow publicly. Privately, they would prefer they did not exist.

---

## ⚡ The Vanguard

**Role in the city:** Former city peacekeepers who went independent after a political split with the Architects. They still keep order — on their terms. Their protection contracts are the closest thing to a legal economy in the city's gray zones.

**Personality:** Disciplined, direct, no-nonsense. They respect competence above all. Earn it and they will die for you. Show weakness and they will move on.

**Aesthetic:** Industrial. Military surplus repurposed. Scarred walls, functional lighting, gear on display. It looks like it has been through something — because it has.

**What they offer players:**
- Best combat gear upgrades
- Protection contracts (highest-paying dungeon runs)
- Combat training dialogue (passive stat insights)

**Reputation unlocks:**
- Trusted: Vanguard-grade weapons, harder contracts with better rewards
- Allied: Access to Vanguard war room, intel on dungeon boss weaknesses, past conflict lore

**Faction motto:** "Order does not maintain itself."

**Relationship with other factions:**
- Architects: Tense alliance. They need each other and resent it equally.
- Hollow: Complicated respect. There is history here — specific NPCs were once Hollow before joining the Vanguard. Neither side forgets.

---

## 🌿 The Hollow

**Role in the city:** Everyone the city forgot — or discarded. They live in the spaces between faction territories. They know more about how the city actually works than anyone, because they have had to learn it to survive.

**Personality:** Warm but guarded. Survivors, not victims. They have built something real in the margins and they are protective of it. They are not bitter — but they remember.

**Aesthetic:** Warm lighting. Found materials. Things repurposed from what others threw away. It should not work as well as it does. That is the point.

**What they offer players:**
- Best prices on everything
- Hidden quests not available through official faction boards
- Underground intel — they know things the other factions do not want known

**Reputation unlocks:**
- Trusted: Significant discounts, hidden quest access, Hollow community lore
- Allied: The hidden entrance to the deepest Hollow market, the city's true history from the people who lived it

**Faction motto:** "We do not own the city. We live in it."

**Relationship with other factions:**
- Architects: Quiet hostility. The Hollow know what the Architects did during the city's founding. The Architects know they know.
- Vanguard: Complicated respect. Some Vanguard members have Hollow roots. Unspoken acknowledgment on both sides.

---

## Faction Interaction Design Rules

1. **No hard locks.** Players can reach Allied with all three factions in v1.
2. **Tension is narrative, not mechanical.** Factions do not block each other's quests — they just react differently to a player who is also working with their rival.
3. **Each faction has a truth.** Allied players learn something about the city's history that neutral players never hear. The three truths connect.
4. **NPC memory is simple in v1.** NPCs know your reputation tier. Full persistent memory is a v2 feature.
